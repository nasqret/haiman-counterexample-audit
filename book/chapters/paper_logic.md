# Dependency graph of the argument

The computational and geometric halves meet only at the regularity comparison.

## Computational half

Lemma 19 is intended to prove that

$$
S_{(133,130,126,122,119,60,60,60)}W
$$

occurs among the minimal generators of $J_8$. Proposition 4 and Theorem 3 then assert a minimal generator of degree 90 and hence a high regularity lower bound.

## Geometric half

Set

$$
X=\operatorname{Proj}(R_8/J_8),\qquad \dim X=63.
$$

Lemma 5 says that an arithmetically Cohen-Macaulay projective variety $S$ with a suitable smooth open subset covered by proper rational curves satisfies

$$
\operatorname{reg}(S)\le \dim S+1.
$$

Proposition 6 supplies that open subset for $X$.

Under the assumption that the affine cone is Cohen-Macaulay, the paper obtains

$$
\operatorname{reg}(X)\le64,
$$

which is supposed to contradict the degree-90 generator.

:::{admonition} Audit warning
:class: warning
The conventions for $\operatorname{reg}(J)$, $\operatorname{reg}(R/J)$, and projective-scheme regularity differ by one in standard usage. The final contradiction will be rewritten with one fixed convention before it is accepted.
:::
