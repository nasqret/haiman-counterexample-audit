# The conjecture and the claimed counterexample

Let $\operatorname{Hilb}^n(\mathbb C^d)$ be the Hilbert scheme of length-$n$ subschemes of affine $d$-space. Its principal component is the closure of the locus of $n$ distinct reduced points.

Lee attributes to Haiman the conjecture that the isospectral Hilbert scheme over this principal component is Cohen-Macaulay. The implication used in the article is that the principal component itself would then be Cohen-Macaulay.

:::{admonition} Target theorem
:class: theorem
The principal component of $\operatorname{Hilb}^9(\mathbb C^8)$ is not locally Cohen-Macaulay at $\mathfrak m^2$.
:::

The point $\mathfrak m^2$ lies in a symmetric affine chart $V_8$. After splitting off translations, the relevant local cone has coordinate ring $R_8/J_8$. The article seeks a minimal generator of $J_8$ in degree 90. Its degree is so large relative to the projective dimension that Cohen-Macaulayness would contradict a regularity bound.

The audit therefore asks two logically distinct questions:

- Does the degree-90 minimal generator really exist?
- If it does, do the geometric hypotheses imply the advertised regularity contradiction?
